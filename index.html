  • B. Pineau, B. Pascal, S. Bourguignon. Unfolded primal-dual algorithm estimating time-varying COVID-19 reproduction numbers.
    Paper    Code   
    Abstract

    Accurately monitoring pathogen transmission during an epidemic is crucial for implementing timely countermeasures and preventing major waves. This task is challenging due to limited prior knowledge about emerging diseases and the low quality of crisis-time data. A key indicator of transmissibility is the time-varying reproduction number, representing the average number of infections caused by an infected individual. This work proposes transforming a variational estimator—designed to remain robust to noisy data and based on a primal-dual optimization algorithm—into a residual convolutional neural network. Trained on synthetic data, the resulting model estimates reproduction numbers from infection counts and demonstrates performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods on real COVID-19 data from multiple countries.
